    • Methodology

    As a Senior User Experience/Product Designer, my methodology is rooted in a user-centered design approach, I employ modern techniques in user research and iterative feedback to shape every stage of the design process. By prioritizing the user's perspective, I ensure that each product aligns with their needs and expectations while remaining intuitive and visually engaging. My approach involves conducting thorough user research, from interviews to competitor analysis, to deeply understand user pain points, motivations, and behaviors. I consistently engage with users, gathering feedback at critical touchpoints, allowing me to validate ideas and refine designs iteratively. Leveraging cutting-edge design tools, rapid prototyping, and rigorous usability testing, I balance functionality with aesthetics to create products that are both effective and enjoyable to use. This process allows me to deliver design solutions that are responsive to the dynamic demands of the modern era, creating seamless, meaningful interactions that drive user satisfaction and product success.

    Research

    In New York City, the Department of Education is represented by two separate yet equally important groups. The students who attend the schools to learn, and the teachers and administrators who teach them.

    In 2021, I was brought on as a Senior UX Designer for the New York City Department of Education—a system with over a million students, countless administrators, and a complex web of stakeholders. My assignment: to design an application unlike anything the NYC DOE had attempted before. It had many moving parts, multiple teams, and the eyes of senior leadership fixated on it. This was uncharted territory, a challenge that would test every design principle and push the boundaries of innovation. The first step? Delve into the research, break down the complexities, and most importantly—understand the people who would rely on it.
